Maurice: A Mediccation Management App

Maurice is a smart device application for visually-impaired folks to manage their medications. The goal of this project is to look at how visually-impaired users find and take their medications, and overall improve their experience. My research and interviews showed that visually-impaired users can sometimes struggle with finding their medications, taking the right dosage, and tracking when medications need to be refilled or when medications have expired. A significant amount of anxiety surrounds taking medications correctly, and the consequences for not doing so could result in hospitalization. However, being able to maintain independence and privacy is an important level of autonomy that many visually-impaired users need. The aim of this application is to provide visually-impaired users with tools for them to independently and safely manage their medications. Maurice assists a visually-impaired user with taking the right medication, the right dosage, and track expiration dates/refills of medications through a combination conversational and visual interface.

Tablet Mount Controller for Ascend

Last year's large team capstone was tasked with creating a game with a mobile element. Ascend uses a tablet as a viewport for the player as well as traditional mouse and keyboard controls, so an elegant way to toggle between devices was necessary to maintain the flow of the game. Using HID compatible Arduino, I prototyped and built this controller to act as both controller and tablet mount.
